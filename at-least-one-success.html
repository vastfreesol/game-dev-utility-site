At least one success

Shows the chance that repeated attempts succeed at least once.

Formula

AtLeastOne = 1 - (1 - p)^n

This page breaks the formula down into what it is for, what each variable means, and whether it depends on any fixed constants.

Context

Why you would use it

Shows the chance that repeated attempts succeed at least once.

Variables

What changes from use to use

p is single-attempt chance. n is number of attempts.

Constants

What stays fixed

1 represents the full probability range.
